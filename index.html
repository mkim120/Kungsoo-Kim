About Me

I was born in South Korea and immigrated with my family to the United States at the age of seven. My birth name is Kungsoo (경수), but I also go by Michael as my "American" name. I have an immense passion and respect for my background and culture, and I strive to maintain and improve my Korean as a first generation immigrant.

Currently, I am an Amazon Seller Support Associate for Korean and English speaking business partners selling on the Amazon platform. While I am able to utilize and hone my Korean language skills through this role, my goal is to change careers into the tech field. After graduating from the University of Pittsburgh with a Bachelor's degree in Information Science, I completed a Back-end Java Development boot camp focusing on Client-Server Programming (SQL, Spring, JPA, REST) as well as covering topics such as Data Structures, Algorithms, Design Patterns, Cloud/Microservice deployments, and DevOps (e.g. CI/CD).

Blog: My Web Dev Journey

By: Michael Kim | Posted: Aug 4, 2023

The First Steps

After I graudated college and started working with Amazon, I didn't get the opportunity to enter the tech industry with my degree in Information Science. However, while working full-time, I took the chance to join a Back-end Java Development boot camp that focused on Client-Server Programming (SQL, Spring, JPA, REST) and covered topics such as Data Structures, Algorithms, Design Patterns, Cloud/Microservice deployments, and DevOps (e.g. CI/CD). This program served as a refresher for a lot of what I had learned in college and as a stepping stone into the world of web and software development.

Composing Code

However, I knew that my passion had always been for Front-end Development and User Interface design. I'd like to attest my interest in coding and design to my love for music and the arts. Since I was a child, I enjoyed playing and listening to music, and I started learning the piano and clarinet during grade school. When I entered high school, I was captivated by the world of percussion and joined the marching band to play keyboard percussion instruments like the marimba and vibraphone. In college, I was an active member of the varsity marching band in the baritone section and also performed in the symphonic band and basketball pep band.

What I've found most interesting in my journey so far is that in music and coding, you're basically learning a new language. We learn to read notes and play them to create a song the same way that we learn syntax and write code to create programs. I've played an instrument for as long as I can remember, and realizing the shared principles between these two seemingly unrelated topics has allowed me to keep my mind open to all of the opportunities that are available within the vast field of technology.

The Next Steps

With this in mind, it's my goal to enter the world of web development, and as I continue to find bridges between my passion for music and tech, I desire to make an impact in the tech industry as a contributor who recognizes the significance of patience and consistency, whether that involves overcoming a difficult piece of music or dealing with a challenging coding problem. Contact me if you also love music and have a passion for technology, and let's discuss!
